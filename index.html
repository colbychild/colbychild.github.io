  • About Me

    I am currently a GIS intern with the state of Utah's Public Lands Policy Coordinating Office. There I am growing my geospatial skillset and enjoy both technical problem solving and the artistic side of GIS including cartography and UI/UX.

    I graduated from the University of Utah in 2022 with a degree in Geography focused on human-environment interaction and a degree in Philosophy of Science focused on environmental science. My studies bridged the divide between human and physical geography and I valued interdisciplinary collaboration. I believe that my unorthodox degree pairing gives me a unique and important skillset in a world that needs more integration of knowledge among specialties. Beyond my technical know-how in the social and physical sciences, I can think critically and communicate specialized knowledge to non-technical audiences.

    In my free time, I enjoy photographing landscapes and identifying plants while out hiking, camping, or skiing.
